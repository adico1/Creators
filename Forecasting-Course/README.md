# Forecasting & Predictive Reasoning

A practical course on how to reason about the future with rigor instead of guesswork. Every module explains not just *what* to do, but *why* it works, so you build durable judgment rather than memorized tricks.

## Module 1: Why Most Predictions Fail
Most forecasts fail for a small number of repeatable reasons: overconfidence in a single story, ignoring base rates, and treating the future as a simple extension of recent trends. This module walks through each failure mode with real examples, and explains the underlying cognitive bias (e.g. availability bias, narrative fallacy) that causes it, so you can recognize the pattern in your own thinking.

## Module 2: Base Rates and Reference Classes
Before predicting a specific outcome, ask: "What usually happens in situations like this?" This module teaches reference class forecasting — finding a category of similar past events and using their outcome distribution as your starting point — and explains the reasoning for why this consistently outperforms intuition-only predictions.

## Module 3: Bayesian Updating
Forecasting is not a one-time guess; it's a process of starting with a prior belief and updating it as new evidence arrives. This module explains the logic of Bayes' theorem in plain language, walks through worked examples of updating a probability estimate step by step, and shows why small, frequent updates beat large, rare ones.

## Module 4: Scenario Planning
Instead of predicting one future, build 3-4 plausible futures (best case, worst case, most likely, wildcard) and reason through the chain of causes that would lead to each. This module explains why holding multiple scenarios in mind reduces the narrative fallacy and improves decision-making under uncertainty.

## Module 5: Calibration — Knowing How Sure to Be
A good forecaster doesn't just predict outcomes, they attach accurate confidence levels. This module explains what calibration means statistically, how to score your own predictions over time (Brier scores), and the reasoning behind why well-calibrated 70% confidence should be right about 7 times out of 10 — no more, no less.

## Module 6: Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
Once you have a forecast with a confidence level, this module explains how to reason through decision trees and expected value calculations, so choices are grounded in the trade-off between probability and consequence, not gut feeling alone.

## How to Use This Course
Work through the modules in order. Each one builds the reasoning tools used in the next. Where possible, apply each technique to a real decision you're currently facing, and write down your reasoning — the goal is to make your thinking process visible and improvable over time.
